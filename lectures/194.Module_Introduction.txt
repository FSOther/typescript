At this point in the course,
you learned quite a bit about TypeScript, its basics
and also some more advanced features already,
and up to this point,
we always worked in relatively simple
basic demo projects and project setups.
Now, of course, it is more realistic
that you might be working on more complex projects
that consist of multiple files,
and that might also not just consist of TypeScript files.
Instead, you also might have HTML, CSS
and all kinds of other files in such projects.
That's why in this section we'll explore
how you can build such TypeScript projects
and how you can in general build
and work with more complex TypeScript projects.
And for that, we'll take a look
beyond the tsc command, the TypeScript Compiler,
which we installed way earlier in the course,
and we'll take a look at alternative build tools.
Most importantly, we'll take a look at Vite.
