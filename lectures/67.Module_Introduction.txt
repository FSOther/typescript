In some JavaScript,

and therefore also TypeScript projects,

you will work with classes.

And therefore in this section,

we'll explore how TypeScript helps you

with working with classes,

what it adds to vanilla JavaScript

when it comes to working with classes.

And we'll also explore the concept of interfaces,

which, unlike classes, is a TypeScript-exclusive feature,

because that's really important.

Classes are a JavaScript feature.

TypeScript, however,

as you will see throughout this section,

enhances this feature

with certain extra features, you could say.

But classes themselves are a JavaScript feature,

just enhanced by TypeScript.

Interfaces, however,

as you will see throughout this section,

are a TypeScript-exclusive feature

that can be used in conjunction with classes, though.
